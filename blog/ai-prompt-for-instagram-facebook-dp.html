Welcome to our ultimate gallery of AI prompts designed specifically for Facebook posts and profile pictures. As AI image generators like Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, and custom face-swapping apps become more advanced, the key to a scroll-stopping photo lies entirely in the text prompt you provide.

In this collection, we focus on highly realistic aesthetics that perform incredibly well on social media. From the nostalgic grain of a 90s disposable camera to the crisp, dramatic lighting of a Vogue editorial cover, these prompts are engineered to give you professional-grade results without stepping foot in a studio.

⚠️ Important: How to Use These Prompts

To keep the prompts below clean and readable, we have removed the repetitive instructions required to map your face to the generation. When copying a prompt into your AI tool, always append the following two elements:

  • Your Identity Command: Add "of me with exact same facial and physical identity from the uploaded image" (or your specific AI tool's equivalent image-referencing command) to ensure the AI uses your face.
  • The Aspect Ratio: Add "--ar 9:16" or "aspect ratio 9:16" at the very end of the prompt. This vertical format is optimized for Facebook mobile feeds and stories.

Pro Tips for Posting AI Art on Facebook

Now that you have your perfect AI-generated portrait, you want to make sure it looks stunning when uploaded to Facebook. Keep these technical details in mind:

  • Combat Compression: Facebook aggressively compresses images. To retain the high detail from these prompts, resize your images to 2048 pixels on the longest edge before uploading.
  • Fixing AI Glitches: If the AI messes up the eyes or fingers in your generated image, don't discard the whole prompt! Keep the exact same prompt but change the "seed" number, or run it through an AI upscaler with a "face restore" feature.
  • Experiment with Colors: The prompts above are highly customizable. Feel free to swap "dark forest-green" with "crimson red," or change "Kodak Portra 400" to "Cinestill 800T" to completely shift the mood of the photograph.
