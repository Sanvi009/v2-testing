Mastering 3-Frame Prompts: Split Screen & Triptychs

Welcome to Promptgalary's definitive guide on creating multi-panel storytelling through AI photography. Designing a split-screen composition or a vertical triptych is one of the most highly sought-after techniques in generative AI art right now. It allows creators to tell a dynamic story, showcase different angles, or create high-end fashion editorials all within a single image generation.

However, generating these images presents a massive challenge: consistency. Most AI models struggle to keep the subject's face, clothing, and environmental lighting identical across three separate frames. Left to its own devices, an AI will often generate three people who look vaguely similar, rather than the exact same person in different poses.

The collection of advanced prompts on this page solves that problem. By using precise structural commands, rigorous lighting cues, and explicit "identity lock" phrasing, these prompts force the AI to maintain absolute continuity across the top, middle, and bottom panels.

How to Use These Triptych Prompts Effectively

Before you copy and paste the prompts below, it's vital to understand how to format them for your specific AI image generator to achieve the best possible results.

For Midjourney Users

  • Aspect Ratios: All of the prompts below are optimized for vertical viewing. Ensure you keep the --ar 9:16 parameter at the very end of your prompt so Midjourney formats the three stacked frames correctly.
  • Character Consistency: If you are using a reference photo of a real person, use Midjourney's Character Reference feature. Add --cref https://forum.wordreference.com/threads/your-picture-picture-of-you.4038579/ --cw 100 to the end of the prompt to lock in the facial features and the clothing.

For DALL-E 3 and ChatGPT Users

  • Upload Your Image: Use the attachment clip to upload your reference photo directly into the chat.
  • Conversational Tweaks: DALL-E 3 may try to rewrite your prompt. You can preface the pasted text with: "Please use this exact prompt without modifying it to generate an image based on my attached photo."

90s Digital Camera Photo Split Screen Prompt for Consistent Faces

Create three vertical split-screen photos with identical faces using 1990s digital camera aesthetics including harsh flash, red eye, and a distinct CCD sensor look.


Deconstructing the Prompt (Why this works): This prompt succeeds because it relies heavily on hardware-specific keywords like "Sony Cyber shot," "CCD sensor look," and "blooming highlights" to force the AI out of its default glossy, plastic style. By combining these hardware terms with strong negative prompts like "no perfect skin" and "avoid soft glam," we ensure the final image retains the messy, nostalgic authenticity of Y2K point-and-shoot photography. The layout commands ("Seamless edge to edge layout. No borders") ensure the 9:16 aspect ratio is divided perfectly without unnatural comic-book-style lines.

By explicitly scripting the camera distance for each separate frame (Top: View from behind, Middle: Extreme close-up, Bottom: Mid-shot), the prompt forces the AI to construct a dynamic, multi-angle narrative of a single moment, preventing the model from just generating the same pose three times.

Deconstructing the Prompt (Why this works): To maximize photorealism, this prompt employs render-engine keywords commonly used in 3D modeling ("Octane render," "Unreal Engine 5," "8K ultra-high resolution"). It also dictates a distinct, logical visual hierarchy: the top frame establishes the subject's identity via a clever side mirror reflection, the middle frame establishes the lifestyle and wardrobe, and the bottom frame grounds the environment. The specific lens parameters ("35mm and 85mm lens combination") give the AI exact instructions on how to handle depth of field and background blur.
