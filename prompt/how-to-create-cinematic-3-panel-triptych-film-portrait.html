How to Create a Cinematic 3-Panel Triptych Film Portrait

AI Image Prompts
Published: 2026-03-20
Author: promptgalary

Learn how to generate an ultra-cinematic portrait composed of three horizontal frames stacked vertically. This AI prompt creates a gritty, analog film storyboard featuring a rainy night atmosphere, strong film grain, and moody street reflections. Ideal for storytelling, cinematic character showcases, and retro noir aesthetics.

Generated Images

The Prompt 

Prompt Ultra cinematic portrait composed of three horizontal frames stacked vertically. me with exact same identity from the uploaded image, wearing a crisp white shirt with a black tie and a dark tailored blazer. Subtle confident smile. Frame 1: low angle shot as i walks slowly through a rainy city street at night, street lights reflecting on wet pavement. Frame 2: side profile while lighting a cigarette under a dim street lamp, rain droplets visible in the air. Frame 3: looking down while adjusting the tie, rain running down his hair. Moody night atmosphere, glowing street reflections, extremely heavy film grain, strong noise, gritty analog film aesthetic. ar 9:16

How to Use This Prompt

To customize this storyboard prompt, you can easily change the narrative by modifying the actions in each panel. Keep the 'three horizontal frames stacked vertically' instruction, but rewrite the descriptions for Frame 1, Frame 2, and Frame 3 to tell a different story. You can shift the wardrobe from a 'crisp white shirt with a black tie' to a 'leather motorcycle jacket' or a 'heavy winter parka'. Alter the environment by replacing the 'rainy city street at night' with a 'foggy forest path' or a 'neon-lit subway station'. Finally, adjust the aesthetic keywords like 'gritty analog film' to 'clean high-fashion editorial' or 'vintage 1970s Polaroid' to completely change the mood.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How do I get the AI to generate exactly three stacked frames?
    Include explicit structural keywords like 'composed of three horizontal frames stacked vertically' or 'triptych storyboard layout' at the very beginning of your prompt. This instructs models like Midjourney or Stable Diffusion to split the canvas before rendering the specific details in the scene.
  • Can I change the specific actions happening in each frame?
    Yes. The prompt is structured specifically to dictate the narrative of each panel. Simply locate 'Frame 1:', 'Frame 2:', and 'Frame 3:' in the text and replace the following action descriptions with whatever scene or story you want to direct.
  • How do I keep my face consistent across all three frames?
    Maintaining exact facial consistency in a single multi-frame generation can be tricky. Use a strong character reference image parameter (like `--cref` in Midjourney) and ensure the description of the character's clothing, hairstyle, and identity remains identical in the base prompt so the AI understands it is the exact same person in all three panels.
