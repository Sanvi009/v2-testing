How to Create a Cinematic Golden Hour Portrait with Exact Face Match

AI Image Prompts
Published: 2026-03-04
Author: promptgalary

Generate a dramatic, moody portrait of yourself in a misty field during golden hour. This prompt ensures facial identity is preserved while creating a cinematic atmosphere with rich colors, soft lighting, and film-like texture.

Generated Images

The Prompt 

A CINEMATIC PORTRAIT OF ME WITH EXACT SAME FACIAL AND PHYSICAL IDENTITY FROM THE UPLOADED IMAGE , STANDING IN A FIELD DURING GOLDEN HOUR WITH DRAMATIC COLORED LIGHTING. THE ATMOSPHERE IS MOODY WITH MIST OR LIGHT FOG, SOFT GRADIENTS OF DEEP BLUE AND WARM ORANGE-RED LIGHT FILLING THE SCENE. A TREE SILHOUETTE IN THE BACKGROUND, DREAMY TONES, SOFT SHADOWS, AND NATURAL FILM-LIKE GRAIN. SIDE PROFILE LIGHTING, EMPHASIZING CONTRAST AND DEPTH. AR-9:16

How to Use This Prompt

Start by uploading a clear, well-lit reference photo of yourself to your AI image generator of choice, such as Midjourney. Copy the link to that uploaded image. Then, paste the link at the very beginning of your prompt field, followed by the full text prompt provided here. The image link acts as a visual anchor, telling the AI to use your specific facial features as the foundation. After running the generation, review the four initial outputs. Look for the image that best captures both your likeness and the desired moody lighting. If the face isn't quite right, select the closest match and use the 'vary' or 'remaster' functions to refine it. You can also adjust the prompt slightly—if the fog is too thick, try changing 'light fog' to 'very subtle mist.' If the colors feel too intense, modify 'dramatic colored lighting' to 'soft, warm golden hour lighting.' The AR-9:16 at the end sets the vertical aspect ratio, perfect for phone wallpapers or Instagram stories, so keep that unless you need a different format. Run multiple generations, tweaking the descriptive words each time until the atmosphere and your likeness feel exactly right.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Why does the AI sometimes change my facial features even with a reference image?
    AI models interpret facial features rather than copying them perfectly. Factors like extreme angles, unusual lighting in your reference photo, or conflicting prompt descriptions can cause drift. For best results, use a front-facing reference photo and avoid describing features that contradict it, like changing eye color or adding facial hair.
  • What's the difference between 'golden hour' and 'dramatic colored lighting' in practice?
    Golden hour typically refers to the warm, soft light just after sunrise or before sunset. Adding 'dramatic colored lighting' tells the AI to intensify and stylize those colors—pushing them toward richer oranges, deeper blues, and more contrast than natural golden hour usually provides, creating a more stylized, cinematic effect.
  • How do I get the mist or fog to look realistic rather than like a photo editing mistake?
    The key is moderation. Terms like 'light fog' or 'subtle mist' work better than just 'fog,' which can produce an opaque, unnatural wall of white. Pairing fog with 'soft gradients' and 'depth' helps the AI understand you want atmospheric depth that recedes naturally into the background, not a flat haze over the entire image.
